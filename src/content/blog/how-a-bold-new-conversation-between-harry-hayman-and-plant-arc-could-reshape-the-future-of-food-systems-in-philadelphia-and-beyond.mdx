Harry Hayman has never been someone who waits for the world to change on its own. Anyone who has watched his work in Philadelphia knows that he moves fast, he thinks big, and he builds partnerships that others only imagine. So when Harry began early conversations with the team at Plant ARC at the University of Pennsylvania, it instantly became clear that something significant was taking shape.

Plant ARC operates at the front line of plant adaptability and resilience research, pushing forward knowledge that could redefine how communities grow food in an era of climate pressure and global uncertainty. Led by Professor Doris Wagner, the team focuses on the deep biological mechanisms that allow plants to respond, survive, and thrive. Their work stretches from advanced epigenetics to practical applications that could strengthen crops, stabilize supply chains, and support regional agriculture. Harry quickly recognized that this scientific powerhouse aligned perfectly with the mission driven work he champions across Philadelphia.
